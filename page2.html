2013 In Review

Since Mik Aoki has taken the helm of the Irish, they have continued to improve each year. Last season was no different. The baseball team had the hottest start since 2006. They beat quality opponents such as VA Tech, OU, UCLA, and Cal Poly. Previous Irish teams would have struggled with opponents such as these, but the 2013 team rose to the occasion and achieve a feat that Notre Dame Baseball had not done since 2006, become ranked in the top 25. With these impressive wins, the Irish reached 15th in the nation. Eventually, the squad's pace began to slow when Big-East play began. Mid week losses and poor play in weekend series led to a poor record and decrease in RPI. The Irish's regional hope were in jeprody. As the season began to whind down, the squad made a push in the Big-East tournament. They proceeded to go 4-0 and made it to the championship where they would play UCONN. Unfortunately, they lost and their regional chances were quickly diminishing. While the season ended, some Irish players found great success in the draft. Big-East Player of the Year, Eric Jagielo, was taken in the First Round with the 25th overall pick. Two other Irish players, Dan Slania and Trey Mancini, were taken in the Fifth and Eighth Rounds respectively.
